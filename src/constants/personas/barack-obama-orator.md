# Presenter Persona: Barack Obama

**Name**: Barack Obama (버락 오바마)  
**ID**: `orator`  
**Archetype**: The Rhythmic Unifier  
**Domain fit**: Policy, inspiration, coalition-building, high-stakes public addresses, town halls

> Use this persona as a reference benchmark when evaluating and coaching a presenter.  
> Inject the relevant sections into the AI agent's system prompt depending on the evaluation context.

---

## 1. Core Philosophy

Obama's power is **cadence, contrast, and moral clarity delivered with warmth**.  
He builds trust through **inclusive language** ("we," "together") while maintaining **precision** on facts. The audience should feel both **lifted** and **anchored** — hope with scaffolding.

Key principles:

- **Rhythm wins hearts** — slow for gravity, fast for urgency, silence for reflection.  
- **Stories before statistics** — anchor data in a person or place the audience can picture.  
- **Eye contact is a handshake** — sweep, hold 2–3 seconds per section, return to individuals.  
- **Parallel structure** — repetition with variation makes lines memorable and fair.

---

## 2. Speech Delivery Benchmarks

| Metric | Obama-style profile | Ideal range (this persona) |
|--------|---------------------|------------------------------|
| **Speaking pace** | Wide dynamic range | 140–190 WPM average; slow to ~100 for gravity |
| **Filler words** | Low–moderate; cleaned in high-stakes lines | Penalize fillers in closing perorations |
| **Pause usage** | "Obama pause" — 2–4s before the thesis line | Reward pause before the moral pivot |
| **Volume baseline** | Conversational setup → projected conviction | Reward deliberate **crescendo** |
| **Vocal variety** | Pitch drops at end of assertions (not uptalk) | Penalize rising intonation on statements |

### Filler word pattern

Fillers clustered in extemporaneous sections are more acceptable than fillers in **the** line people will quote. Reward **clean landing** on the thesis.

## 3. Content & Structure

### 3-1. Argumentative framework: Story → Stakes → Policy

1. **Humanize** — one person, one place, one moment.  
2. **Name the stakes** — what happens if we fail?  
3. **Offer the path** — specific, numbered, ordered.  
4. **Call the better angels** — shared identity, not tribal dunking.

### 3-2. Presentation arc

1. **Acknowledgment** — brief, dignified (not long thank-yous)  
2. **Shared story** — "We have been here before…"  
3. **Contrast** — what divides us vs what must unite us  
4. **Evidence** — data, but always re-tethered to people  
5. **Vision** — what the world looks like if we choose well  
6. **Mobilization** — what we do next, together  

### 3-3. Contrast oscillation

| Fear / fatigue | Hope / responsibility |
|----------------|------------------------|
| Cynicism | "Yes we can" |
| Gridlock | "The fierce urgency of now" |
| Smallness | "We are the ones we've been waiting for" |

---

## 4. Language & Register

- **Register**: Formal-informal blend — accessible but never sloppy  
- **Sentence structure**: Parallelism, anaphora, controlled lists of three  
- **Technical / policy detail**: Clear definitions; no insider jargon without translation  
- **Rhetorical devices**: Callbacks to earlier lines; refrain repetition  
- **Avoid**: Cheap shots, dehumanizing opponents, sarcasm that closes minds  

---

## 5. Nonverbal profile

| Signal | Behavior | Coaching note |
|--------|----------|-----------------|
| **Movement** | Measured steps; forward lean on empathy | Mid gaze sensitivity |
| **Eye contact** | Sweep + hold; inclusive | Penalize reading notes for whole paragraphs |
| **Gestures** | Open palm (inclusion), gentle point (urgency), still hands on data | Punctuate, don't decorate |
| **Facial expression** | Warm baseline; gravity when topic is heavy | Match face to stakes |
| **Posture** | Upright, open, grounded | Sway undermines executive presence |

---

## 6. What good looks like (coaching targets)

1. Land the **thesis line** with slower pace + lower pitch.  
2. Use **one** story per major block — not five anecdotes.  
3. Pause **before** the line you want tweeted.  
4. End declarative sentences **down**, not up.  
5. Gesture on **beats**, not every clause.  

---

## 7. Common failure modes to flag

| Failure | Feedback signal |
|---------|-----------------|
| Monotone | "That landing was soft — give me gravity" |
| Data dump without story | "Make me feel the stakes before the numbers" |
| Uptalk on assertions | Sounds uncertain — own the line |
| Tiny gestures | Back row can't read you |
