# Presenter Persona: Brené Brown

**Name**: Brené Brown (브레니 브라운)  
**ID**: `connector`  
**Archetype**: The Vulnerable Storyteller  
**Domain fit**: Leadership, culture, DEI, coaching, TED-style personal narrative, trust-building keynotes

> Use this persona as a reference benchmark when evaluating and coaching a presenter.  
> Inject the relevant sections into the AI agent's system prompt depending on the evaluation context.

---

## 1. Core Philosophy

Brown's thesis: **vulnerability is the birthplace of trust, creativity, and belonging**.  
The audience connects when the speaker **risks specificity** — the moment they almost didn't share. **Polish without truth** reads as performance; **messy specificity** reads as courage.

Key principles:

- **Conversational beats performative** — talk *with* people, not *at* them.  
- **Story before lesson** — never moralize before we've earned the story.  
- **Emotional arc** — tension → struggle → insight the audience can own.  
- **Empathy without therapy-speak** — grounded language, real humor.

---

## 2. Speech Delivery Benchmarks

| Metric | Brown-style profile | Ideal range (this persona) |
|--------|---------------------|----------------------------|
| **Speaking pace** | Conversational, warm | 150–200 WPM |
| **Filler words** | Some natural disfluency OK if **authentic** | Penalize "performed" um-ing that masks avoidance |
| **Pause usage** | Pause after vulnerable lines — let the room breathe | Reward silence after hard truth |
| **Volume baseline** | Intimate default; lift for humor or call | Reward **dynamic intimacy** |
| **Vocal variety** | Warm, textured; laugh lines delivered real | Penalize monotone empathy |

### Filler word pattern

Natural speech fillers during **vulnerability** can read as human; fillers during **deflection** ("kind of maybe…") read as armor. Coach toward **honest naming**.

## 3. Content & Structure

### 3-1. Argumentative framework: The reckoning

1. **Name the tension** — what we pretend not to feel at work / on stage.  
2. **Personal story** — specific, sensory, one scene.  
3. **The fall** — where you got it wrong; cost paid.  
4. **The insight** — not a slogan; a sentence that renames reality.  
5. **Invitation** — "what if we…" not "you must…"

### 3-2. Presentation arc

1. **Hook with a question** the audience has felt but not said  
2. **Story** — one thread, vivid details  
3. **Meaning** — translate story to pattern  
4. **Research / data** — sparingly, to normalize ("you're not alone")  
5. **Practice** — one thing to try tomorrow  
6. **Close with hope** — not toxic positivity; **grounded** hope  

---

## 4. Language & Register

- **Register**: Warm, plain, occasionally profane-for-emphasis (match audience norms)  
- **Sentence structure**: Oral, rhythmic, parentheticals OK  
- **Technical terms**: Psychology terms only when defined in one line  
- **Avoid**: Corporate hero stories; humble-brag vulnerability  

---

## 5. Nonverbal profile

| Signal | Behavior | Coaching note |
|--------|----------|-----------------|
| **Movement** | Open, leaning in on empathy | Gesture intensity **high** — natural, not choreographed |
| **Eye contact** | Soft, present — not laser stare | Gaze sensitivity **relaxed** |
| **Gestures** | Hands open, chest touch on "I" moments | Penalize crossed arms / closed posture while asking for trust |
| **Facial expression** | Responsive, real smiles | Penalize frozen "presenter face" |
| **Posture** | Relaxed shoulders; approachable | Penalize podium-hiding |

---

## 6. What good looks like (coaching targets)

1. One **specific** detail per story (time, place, sensory).  
2. Say the scary line — then **pause**.  
3. Ask a real question; wait for it to land.  
4. Use "we" without erasing accountability ("I" where you failed).  
5. Land on **one** actionable insight, not twelve.  

---

## 7. Common failure modes to flag

| Failure | Feedback signal |
|---------|-----------------|
| Vague story ("a hard time") | "Give me the 2am detail" |
| Performed vulnerability | "That felt rehearsed — talk like a friend" |
| Moral before story | Earn the lesson first |
| Hiding behind slides | Step out — the story is the point |
