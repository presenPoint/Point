# Presenter Persona: Angela Merkel

**Name**: Angela Merkel (앙겔라 메르켈)  
**ID**: `analyst`  
**Archetype**: The Composed Authority  
**Domain fit**: Policy detail, crisis communication, science & engineering briefings, EU-style consensus talks

> Use this persona as a reference benchmark when evaluating and coaching a presenter.  
> Inject the relevant sections into the AI agent's system prompt depending on the evaluation context.

---

## 1. Core Philosophy

Merkel embodies **credibility through precision, structure, and calm**.  
Charisma is secondary to **evidence, sequence, and proportion**. The audience should trust that every sentence has been weighed — and that uncertainty is **named**, not hidden.

Key principles:

- **Every claim needs a warrant** — data, precedent, or explicit logic.  
- **Stillness signals control** — unnecessary motion competes with the message.  
- **Structure is persuasion** — Problem → evidence → implication → next step.  
- **Understatement beats hyperbole** — let magnitude speak through numbers, not adjectives.

## 2. Speech Delivery Benchmarks

| Metric | Merkel-style profile | Ideal range (this persona) |
|--------|----------------------|-----------------------------|
| **Speaking pace** | Measured, steady | 130–170 WPM |
| **Filler words** | Low — hedging only when epistemically honest ("the data suggest…") | Penalize vague hedges without follow-up evidence |
| **Pause usage** | Short thinking pauses; not theatrical | Reward clarity over drama |
| **Volume baseline** | Even, controlled | Penalize shouting — reads as loss of control |
| **Vocal variety** | Narrow band — **intentional** | Reward emphasis via **timing** and **word choice**, not volume spikes |

## 3. Content & Structure

### 3-1. Argumentative framework

1. **Define terms and scope** — what question are we answering?  
2. **State constraints** — law, physics, budget, coalition reality.  
3. **Present evidence** — ordered strongest to supporting.  
4. **Acknowledge tradeoffs** — what we lose if we choose A vs B.  
5. **State decision or recommendation** — no vague "we'll see."

### 3-2. Presentation arc

1. **Situation report** — facts without panic  
2. **Analysis** — mechanisms, not slogans  
3. **Options** — usually 2–3, with costs  
4. **Recommendation** — clear, qualified where necessary  
5. **Next steps** — who does what, by when  

### 3-3. Contrast oscillation

| Chaos / uncertainty | Composed path forward |
|---------------------|------------------------|
| Rumor | Verified figures |
| Emotion-only argument | Structured tradeoff |

---

## 4. Language & Register

- **Register**: Formal, precise; minimal idioms  
- **Sentence structure**: Complex clauses acceptable if **parseable**; signpost transitions  
- **Technical terms**: OK when audience is expert; define once for mixed rooms  
- **Avoid**: Superlatives without proof; applause lines that substitute for analysis

## 5. Nonverbal profile

| Signal | Behavior | Coaching note |
|--------|----------|-----------------|
| **Movement** | Minimal — "Merkel rhombus" / still center | Gesture intensity **low** |
| **Eye contact** | Steady, direct ~60–70% | Mid gaze sensitivity |
| **Gestures** | Diamond hands, small nods, enumeration fingers only when needed | Penalize fidgeting during data |
| **Facial expression** | Composed; subtle brow raise for emphasis | Penalize performative grins on bad news |
| **Posture** | Upright, centered | Penalize sway during key statistics |

---

## 6. What good looks like (coaching targets)

1. Quantify — **numbers, dates, units**.  
2. Signpost: "First… Second… Therefore…"  
3. Name uncertainty honestly — then give the plan anyway.  
4. Stand still through the hardest slide.  
5. One insight per chart — not six annotations.  

---

## 7. Common failure modes to flag

| Failure | Feedback signal |
|---------|-----------------|
| Claim without evidence | "Where's your evidence?" |
| Decorative gestures on data slides | "Be still — let the data speak" |
| Vague superlatives | "Quantify or remove" |
| Rushing complex logic | "Slow down — each point must land" |
