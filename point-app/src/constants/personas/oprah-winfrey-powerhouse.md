# Presenter Persona: Oprah Winfrey

**Name**: Oprah Winfrey (오프라 윈프리)  
**ID**: `powerhouse`  
**Archetype**: The Arena Connector  
**Domain fit**: Stadium keynotes, philanthropy launches, mass-audience inspiration, broadcast-level energy

> Use this persona as a reference benchmark when evaluating and coaching a presenter.  
> Inject the relevant sections into the AI agent's system prompt depending on the evaluation context.

---

## 1. Core Philosophy

Oprah's brand is **moral clarity at scale** — intimacy replicated for thousands.  
She uses **dynamic voice**, **big physicality**, and **direct address** so the back row feels seen. Energy is not volume alone; it is **intentional contrast** (whisper → thunder).

Key principles:

- **Own the stage** — every step is a sentence.  
- **Voice as instrument** — range, not one setting.  
- **Gestures must read from row Z** — size matches venue.  
- **Every person feels spoken to** — micro-moments of eye contact + inclusive language.

---

## 2. Speech Delivery Benchmarks

| Metric | Oprah-style profile | Ideal range (this persona) |
|--------|---------------------|----------------------------|
| **Speaking pace** | Fast default with **dramatic** slows | 160–220 WPM with intentional dips |
| **Filler words** | Low in climax segments | Penalize fillers in **charge** (final CTA) |
| **Pause usage** | Theatrical — audience participation beats | Reward crowd rhythm; hold for applause |
| **Volume baseline** | Wide — whisper to projection | Reward **contrast** as craft |
| **Vocal variety** | Very high — storytelling + sermon + conversation | Penalize single-register energy |

---

## 3. Content & Structure

### 3-1. Argumentative framework: Testimony → Truth → Charge

1. **Witness** — story that implicates shared values  
2. **Truth** — name the pattern ("this is what was really happening")  
3. **Lift** — what becomes possible when we see clearly  
4. **Charge** — specific action, time-bound, emotional  

### 3-2. Presentation arc

1. **Bang opening** — bold line or silence that commands attention  
2. **Heart** — narrative spine  
3. **Turn** — the lesson that hurts a little  
4. **Hope** — agency restored  
5. **Call** — "So here's what I want you to do…"  

### 3-3. Contrast oscillation

| Small / stuck | Big / possible |
|---------------|----------------|
| Shame | "You get a car!" energy (metaphorically — earned release) |
| Private pain | Public purpose |

---

## 4. Language & Register

- **Register**: From kitchen-table intimate to **pulpit clarity** — controlled escalation  
- **Sentence structure**: Repetition for lift; direct second-person address  
- **Technical terms** — only if democratized immediately  
- **Avoid**: Apologizing for taking space; shrinking before the ask  

---

## 5. Nonverbal profile

| Signal | Behavior | Coaching note |
|--------|----------|-----------------|
| **Movement** | Walks the stage; uses depth | Gesture intensity **very high** |
| **Eye contact** | Holds beats; finds individuals | Gaze sensitivity **relaxed** but **big** presence |
| **Gestures** | Wide arms, clap, point to sections of room | Penalize small "indoor voice" gestures in big rooms |
| **Facial expression** | Radiant on release moments | Match intensity to content |
| **Posture** | Open, forward, commanding | Penalize hiding behind podium |

---

## 6. What good looks like (coaching targets)

1. **Whisper one line** everyone leans in for — then **project** the thesis.  
2. Gesture to **sections** of the audience ("you… and you…").  
3. Climax: **zero** filler; every word lands.  
4. End on a **verb** — do something, not "thank you for your time."  

---

## 7. Common failure modes to flag

| Failure | Feedback signal |
|---------|-----------------|
| Playing small | "I can't feel you from the back row" |
| Monotone | "Contrast is your instrument" |
| Tiny gestures | "Size for the venue" |
| Weak ending | "Charge me — what do I do Monday?" |
